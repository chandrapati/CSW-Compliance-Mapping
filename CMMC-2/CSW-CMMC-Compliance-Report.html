Cisco Secure Workload — CMMC 2.0 Compliance Mapping

How to read this report

This report describes how Cisco Secure Workload (CSW) supports a Defense Industrial Base (DIB) organisation’s posture against CMMC 2.0 (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification). Default depth is written for Level 2 because that’s where the technical substance — and the bulk of CSW alignment — lives. Level 1 and Level 3 deltas are called out where the work materially changes.

Why CMMC and not just NIST 800-171? CMMC 2.0 doesn’t invent new controls. It is the DoD’s certification mechanism that your organisation has implemented the controls already required under DFARS 252.204-7012:

  • Level 1 (Foundational, 15 safeguards) — implements FAR 52.204-21. Self-attested annually. Required for FCI.
  • Level 2 (Advanced, 110 controls) — implements all 110 controls of NIST SP 800-171 Rev 2. Self-assessed (some contracts) or third-party-assessed via a C3PAO every 3 years. Required for CUI.
  • Level 3 (Expert, ~134 controls) — Level 2 plus selected NIST SP 800-172 enhancements. Government-led assessment.

Companion document. The accompanying CMMC 2.0 Technical Runbook walks through CUI scope labelling, per-family implementation steps for Level 2, and the Level 1 / Level 3 deltas. Read this report for the audit-narrative view; read the runbook for the deployment view.

Important. CMMC Level 2 assessment is performed by a Certified Third-Party Assessor Organisation (C3PAO). Nothing in this report substitutes for the System Security Plan (SSP), Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M), or the C3PAO engagement. CSW supplies the technical evidence the assessment depends on; the SSP documents the process around that evidence.

1. Executive Summary

CMMC 2.0 final rule (DFARS 252.204-7021 / 32 CFR Part 170) became effective December 16, 2024. DoD contracts will phase in CMMC requirements over the subsequent three years. For the bulk of the DIB, the operative target is Level 2, which implements all 110 controls of NIST SP 800-171 Rev 2.

CSW provides direct technical coverage for six of NIST 800-171’s fourteen control families (AC, AU, CM, RA, SC, SI), supports four more (CA, IA, IR evidence, MA), and is explicitly out of scope for four (AT, MP, PE, PS).

What CSW changes for a CMMC Level 2 programme:

  • CUI Scope Boundary (foundational). Per-enclave segmentation with deny-by-default at the workload identity layer. Workload labels (cui_scope, cui_role, contract_id, enclave) make the boundary enforceable and auditable.
  • AC family. Workload-level access enforcement (3.1.1), inter-enclave information-flow control (3.1.3), external connection verification (3.1.20), publicly-accessible system inventory (3.1.22).
  • AU family. Per-flow + per-process telemetry forwarded to SIEM (3.3.1), action traceability via process attribution + SIEM identity correlation (3.3.2).
  • CM family. Daily configuration baseline per workload (3.4.1), drift detection with change-ticket attribution (3.4.3 / 3.4.4), least-functionality review via 90-day-no-flow port reports (3.4.6).
  • RA family. Continuous CVE inventory per CUI workload (3.11.2), patch SLA tracking and POA&M linkage (3.11.3), compensating- control register for deferred patches.
  • SC family. Per-enclave segmentation with deny-by-default (3.13.1, 3.13.6), public-component scope isolation (3.13.5), ADM-derived as-observed architecture diagrams (3.13.2).
  • SI family. Behavioural detection rules (3.14.2, 3.14.6), conversation-graph anomaly detection (3.14.6), threat-intel- enriched CVE prioritisation (3.14.3), unauthorised-use detection (3.14.7).

What CSW does not change. CSW does not author the SSP or POA&M, does not perform the C3PAO assessment, does not address AT (training), MP (media), PE (physical), or PS (personnel). Identity is upstream. CSW is the technical evidence engine the SSP documents around.

5. Suggested Next Steps

For DIB organisations targeting CMMC Level 2 with CSW:

  1. Confirm CUI scope first. The CUI scope determination is the single most consequential decision in a CMMC engagement. The wider the scope, the larger the assessment. Get the CUI-handling owner to sign off the scope before deploying CSW labels.
  1. Pilot on the AC + SC families. Workload-level segmentation per enclave is the foundation everything else builds on. The simulation→enforcement workflow gives you weeks to refine without breaking systems.
  2. Wire CSW telemetry into the SIEM you already have. AU.L2- 3.3.1 / 3.3.2 expect logs in your SIEM with action traceability. CSW supplies the workload-tier feed; the SIEM is where correlation and retention live.
  3. Tie drift detection to your change-management ticketing day one. CM.L2-3.4.3 / 3.4.4 expect change tracking; orphan changes (CSW-detected, ticket-absent) become unauthorised-change candidates that the C3PAO will ask about.
  4. Link CSW vuln IDs to POA&M items. RA.L2-3.11.3 expects a tracked remediation programme; the POA&M is the artefact. CSW produces the per-workload CVE feed; cross-reference into POA&M so the assessment trail is contiguous.
  5. Build the SSP appendix once. The SSP describes how each practice is implemented; CSW evidence (workspace exports, baseline exports, CVE lists, dossier templates) sits in the appendix referenced from each practice section. Build the appendix structure once and refresh per cycle.
  6. Plan SME review. This document is a v1 mapping. Before being relied upon in a formal C3PAO engagement, route to a CMMC Registered Practitioner Organisation (RPO) for assessment guidance currency and to a current Cisco Secure Workload SE for product-capability accuracy.

6. Disclaimer

This report and its companion runbook describe how Cisco Secure Workload capabilities can support a CMMC 2.0 programme at Level 2 (with Level 1 and Level 3 deltas). They are not legal or compliance advice and do not constitute a finding of compliance.

Specifically:

  • The DoD publishes the authoritative CMMC 2.0 text at https://dodcio.defense.gov/CMMC/. The DCMA Defense Industrial Base Cybersecurity Assessment Center (DIBCAC) and the Cyber AB publish authoritative assessment guidance. Always validate against the current effective rule (DFARS 252.204-7021 / 32 CFR Part 170) and against any DoD class deviations.
  • CMMC Level 2 assessment is performed by a Certified Third-Party Assessor Organisation (C3PAO) or, for limited contracts, by self-assessment. Nothing in this document substitutes for the SSP, POA&M, or the C3PAO engagement.
  • Mapping a CSW capability to a 800-171 practice does not by itself satisfy the practice. The C3PAO will look for documented process, evidence cadence, and management review behind the technical implementation.
  • CSW does not address AT, MP, PE, or PS families. Reference your HR, media-handling, physical-protection, and personnel-security programmes for those.

This document should receive subject-matter-expert review for both CMMC 2.0 currency and current Cisco product capability before being used in a customer engagement.
