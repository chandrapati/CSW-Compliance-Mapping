Executive Summary & CSW Role

FIPS 140-2/3 is the US federal standard for cryptographic module validation. Cisco Secure Workload (CSW) is not itself a cryptographic module — it does not perform encryption. However, CSW plays a critical enforcement and detection role in FIPS compliance programs by detecting non-FIPS-compliant protocols, enforcing approved communication paths, auditing cryptographic service access, and defining the network boundary around FIPS-validated infrastructure.

This document is scoped exclusively to areas where CSW meaningfully contributes to FIPS compliance. Cryptographic module validation (CMVP), key management, and algorithm implementation remain outside CSW scope and require FIPS-validated hardware and software components.

FIPS Boundary Architecture

CSW defines and enforces the network boundary around FIPS-validated cryptographic infrastructure:

  • Create dedicated FIPS-Boundary scope containing HSM cluster, crypto services, and KMS endpoints

  • Apply default-deny inbound policy — only explicitly approved workloads may access crypto services

  • Log all connections to FIPS-Boundary scope with full process context for compliance audit

  • Alert on any unapproved access attempt to FIPS-validated infrastructure
