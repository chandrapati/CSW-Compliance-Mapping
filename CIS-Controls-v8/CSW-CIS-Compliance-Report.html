Cisco Secure Workload — CIS Controls v8.1 Compliance Mapping

How to read this report

This report describes how Cisco Secure Workload (CSW) supports an organisation’s posture against the CIS Critical Security Controls v8.1 at the workload tier. Default depth is written for Implementation Group 2 (IG2), with IG1 and IG3 deltas called out where the work materially changes.

Why CIS Controls and not just NIST 800-53. The CIS Controls are an operationally ordered, prioritised subset of what NIST 800-53 describes. Where 800-53 lists 1,000+ controls, CIS compresses to 18 Controls and ~153 Safeguards, ranked by what defends against actual attacks first. Many programmes use CIS as the “what to do this year” view and 800-53 as the “what to satisfy in the contract” view.

Companion document. The accompanying CIS Controls v8.1 Technical Runbook walks through deployment, scope labelling per IG, and the per-Safeguard implementation steps. Read this report for the audit-narrative view; read the runbook for the deployment view.

1. Executive Summary

CIS Controls v8.1 organises 18 Controls and ~153 Safeguards by Implementation Group (IG1 / IG2 / IG3). CSW directly addresses six of the 18 Controls and supports another seven; the remaining five are explicitly outside CSW’s scope (training, email/web, backup, and similar non-workload functions).

What CSW changes for a CIS Controls programme:

  • Control 1 / 2 — Inventory. Continuous, agent-based workload inventory and per-workload software inventory, refreshed at the cadence the IG band expects (monthly for IG1, weekly for IG2, continuous for IG3). Cloud connectors capture ephemeral assets even without an agent.
  • Control 4 — Secure Configuration. Daily configuration baseline per workload (OS, packages, listening ports, processes) with drift detection and change-ticket attribution.
  • Control 7 — Continuous Vulnerability Management. Per-workload CVE inventory with CVSS, EPSS, and exploit-availability context, prioritised by reachability in the actual conversation graph (not just CVSS score).
  • Control 8 — Audit Log Management. Per-flow + per-process audit detail forwarded to your SIEM, retained per the 90-day IG2 minimum (or longer per overlapping framework requirements).
  • Control 13 — Network Monitoring and Defense. The densest CSW fit. East-west visibility, segmentation enforcement, behavioural detection, conversation-graph anomalies, and SIEM forwarding — the substrate for Safeguards 13.1 through 13.10.
  • Control 17 — Incident Response. Six-artefact reconstruction bundle for every declared incident, ready to feed playbooks and post-incident review (Safeguard 17.8).

What CSW does not change. CSW does not address Controls 5 (Account Management), 9 (Email & Web Browser), 11 (Data Recovery), 14 (Security Awareness), or the HR/physical sub-Safeguards inside other Controls. Reference your identity, email/web, backup, and training stacks for those.

5. Suggested Next Steps

For organisations adopting or maturing CIS Controls posture with CSW:

  1. Confirm your IG target. IG1 / IG2 / IG3 calibration drives inventory cadence, scan cadence, log retention, and segmentation depth. Get this signed off by security leadership before scoping.
  1. Pilot on Controls 1, 2, 7, 13. These are the densest CSW alignment and produce the fastest visible wins. Inventory and software inventory are the foundation; Controls 7 and 13 are where the daily security work lives.
  2. Tie drift detection to your change-management ticketing day one. Orphan changes (Safeguard 4.1) are one of the most asked-for evidence types in CIS assessment, and CSW produces the feed automatically.
  3. Pair Control 13 with the SIEM you already have. CSW does not replace the SIEM; it feeds richer, workload-tier data. The uplift to Safeguards 13.1 / 13.3 / 13.6 comes from forwarding CSW telemetry to the SIEM you already operate.
  4. Build the Control 17 incident dossier template once. The six-artefact bundle is reusable across every incident; standardise the format so SOC analysts produce identical artefacts under pressure.
  5. Plan SME review. This document is a v1 mapping. Before being relied upon in a formal compliance engagement, route to a CIS Controls assessment specialist for currency and to a current Cisco Secure Workload SE for product-capability accuracy.

6. Disclaimer

This report and its companion runbook describe how Cisco Secure Workload capabilities can support a CIS Critical Security Controls v8.1 programme at the workload tier. They are not legal or compliance advice and do not constitute a finding of compliance.

Specifically:

  • CIS publishes the authoritative Safeguard text and Implementation Group mapping at https://www.cisecurity.org/. Always validate against the current effective version of the Controls (v8.1 at the time of authoring; subsequent versions and minor revisions occur).
  • Mapping a Safeguard to a CSW capability does not by itself satisfy the Safeguard. Safeguards expect documented process, evidence cadence, and management review — CSW supplies the technical layer that those process requirements depend on.
  • CSW does not address Controls 5, 9, 11, 14 or the HR/physical sub-Safeguards within other Controls. Reference your identity, email/web, backup, and training stacks for those.

This document should receive subject-matter-expert review for both CIS Controls accuracy and current Cisco product capability before being used in a customer engagement.
